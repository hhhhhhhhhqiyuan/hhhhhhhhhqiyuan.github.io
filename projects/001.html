Acacia Outdoor Website

A commercial product page system for Space Acacia, built around premium outdoor comfort, product education, responsive shopping, and e-commerce conversion.

Open live site View design system
theacacia.com / Space Acacia

Brief

Make outdoor gear feel modern, comfortable, and easy to shop.

The site presents Acacia's tent, floor, canopy, accessories, bundles, and promotional shopping paths. The live homepage currently highlights a Costco Roadshow campaign, product education, press hits, and conversion-supporting service messages.

My Role

Commercial site direction and e-commerce experience.

This case study frames the work as product page architecture: hero shopping module, SKU configuration, feature education, proof content, video, reviews, cross-sell, and Shopify-ready conversion moments.

Shopping System

A product-first interface that explains before it sells.

The page starts with a recognizable Shopify product flow: image gallery, price, reviews, model selection, size, color, accessories, shipping promises, and cart actions. The goal is to keep decision-making visible without making the page feel overloaded.

Hero gallery anchors the product visually before long-form storytelling begins. Variant controls turn model, size, and color into a guided decision path. Shipping, warranty, and financing signals reduce purchase friction early.

Commerce Flow

How the page moves a shopper from curiosity to purchase.

01Orient

Product name, price, reviews, gallery, and core promise appear immediately.

02Configure

Model, size, color, and accessory modules support comparison and choice.

03Educate

Feature sections explain comfort, weatherproofing, materials, setup, and details.

04Convert

Reviews, video, cross-sell, email capture, and add-to-cart complete the shopping loop.

Story Modules

Feature education is treated like a campaign page.

Instead of relying only on product specs, the page uses editorial blocks and outdoor imagery to translate functional details into a more emotional buying story: comfort, durability, weatherproofing, and fast setup.

Dark and light sections create rhythm across a long product page. Large headlines turn technical benefits into memorable product claims. Image-led sections make the tent feel like a lifestyle system, not a SKU.

Retention Layer

The lower page turns proof into action.

After the product story, the page shifts into credibility and next steps: details, video, press/customer proof, recommendations, community signup, and footer navigation. This makes the product page work as both a sales page and a brand surface.

Video and review content give shoppers evidence after the feature story. Recommended products support basket expansion and category discovery. Community signup keeps the page useful even when a visitor is not ready to buy.
